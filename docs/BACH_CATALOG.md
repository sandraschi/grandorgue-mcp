# J.S. Bach — Complete Organ Works Reference

Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) is the supreme master of the organ. His organ works are the foundation of the instrument's repertoire — every organist, from beginner to virtuoso, studies and performs Bach. This catalog lists all works suitable for GrandOrgue performance.

### Trio Sonatas (BWV 525-530)

The six Trio Sonatas are the pinnacle of three-part organ writing. Each hand plays an independent melodic line while the feet play the bass.

### Orgelbuchlein (Little Organ Book, BWV 599-644)

Forty-five short chorale preludes for the church year. These are the essential teaching pieces — compact, beautiful, each a perfect miniature.

Key entries:
- BWV 599: "Nun komm, der Heiden Heiland" (Advent)
- BWV 608: "In dulci jubilo" (Christmas)
- BWV 622: "O Mensch, bewein dein Sunde gross" (Passion) — the most expressive
- BWV 639: "Ich ruf zu dir, Herr Jesu Christ" (General)
- BWV 641: "Wenn wir in hochsten Noten sein" (General)

### Schubler Chorales (BWV 645-650)

Six chorale preludes published by Bach himself in 1748-49, transcribed from cantata movements:

| BWV | Title | Occasion |

### The Great Eighteen Chorales (BWV 651-668)

Bach's final and most profound collection of chorale preludes, compiled near the end of his life.

Must-play entries:
- BWV 651: "Komm, Heiliger Geist" — Fantasia, monumental
- BWV 654: "Schmucke dich, o liebe Seele" — the most beautiful
- BWV 659: "Nun komm, der Heiden Heiland" — meditative trio
- BWV 668: "Vor deinen Thron tret ich" — Bach's deathbed chorale

### Clavierubung III (BWV 669-689, 552, 802-805)

The "German Organ Mass" — a systematic collection of chorale settings for the Lutheran liturgy framed by the monumental "St. Anne" Prelude and Fugue (BWV 552).

### Canonic Variations (BWV 769)

Five canonic variations on "Vom Himmel hoch" — Bach's most contrapuntally dense organ work.

### Passacaglia and Fugue in C minor (BWV 582)

One of the greatest sets of variations ever written. The passacaglia theme repeats 20 times over a ground bass, building to an enormous fugue. ~15 minutes, requires full organ registration with 32' pedal reed.

### Pastorale in F major (BWV 590)

A gentle, pastoral four-movement work — rare in Bach's output for its relaxed, bucolic character. Perfect for a flute registration.

## Playing Bach on GrandOrgue

### Registration Principles

**North German Baroque organs** (Schnitger, Silbermann):
- Clear Principal choruses on each manual
- The plenum (full organ) as the default registration for Preludes and Fugues
- Reed stops for solo cantus firmus lines in chorale settings
- Pedal: 16' + 8' + 4' + Mixture + 16' Reed for big moments

**Central German organs** (Thuringia, Saxony):
- More colorful flue stops
- Contrasting manuals for echo effects
- Use the Rugpositiv or Oberwerk for lighter textures

**Recommended registration for BWV 565 (D minor Toccata)**:
```
Great:     Principal 8', Octave 4', Superoctave 2', Mixture, Trompete 8'
Swell:     Gamba 8', Flute 4' (for the fugue episodes)
Pedal:     Subbass 16', Octave 8', Posaune 16'
```

## MIDI Files

Complete MIDI recordings of Bach organ works are available from:

- **Kunst der Fuge**: https://www.kunstderfuge.com/ — largest classical MIDI archive
- **Classical Archives**: https://www.classicalarchives.com/midi/
- **Mutopia Project**: https://www.mutopiaproject.org/

These MIDI files can be played through GrandOrgue by:
1. Downloading the MIDI file
2. Opening it in GrandOrgue's built-in MIDI player (Panels → MIDI Recorder)
3. Configuring the MIDI channels to match your organ's manuals

With GrandOrgue MCP, an agent can queue and perform Bach MIDI files automatically.

## Programming Note for Agents

When an LLM agent is asked to "play some Bach," the recommended workflow:

1. Query the Bach catalog (this document) to find a suitable piece
2. Find the piece on Kunst der Fuge or in the local MIDI collection
3. Load the MIDI file into GrandOrgue via `go_send_sysex` or the built-in recorder
4. Set an appropriate registration with `go_set_stop` calls
5. Play the piece through GrandOrgue's audio engine

A future tool, `go_play_bwv(number)`, will automate this entire pipeline.
